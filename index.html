📘 6‑Month Melbourne High Mastery Program

Months 1–2 — Foundation

  • Build reading stamina (3–4 sessions/week)
  • Core maths practice (2 sets/week + corrections)
  • Reasoning basics (2 sets/week)
  • 1 writing piece/week
  • Focus on routine and consistency

Months 3–4 — Development

  • Harder selective maths (3 sets/week, 1 timed)
  • Timed reasoning sets
  • Writing + rewrites with improvements
  • Start past paper sections (1/week)

Month 5 — Pressure Training

  • Timed maths, reading, reasoning sections
  • 2 timed writing tasks/week
  • 1 full mock exam/week

Month 6 — Exam Simulation

  • 2 full mock exams/week
  • Only light revision + mistake correction
  • No new content, focus on sleep + calm

🧪 Full Mock Exam (Maths • Reading • Writing • Reasoning)

Use this as a full exam simulation at home.

  • Section A — Maths: 20 questions, 40 minutes
  • Section B — Reading: 10 questions, 30 minutes
  • Section C — Writing: 1 of 3 prompts, 20 minutes
  • Section D — Reasoning: 20 questions, 30 minutes

Maths covers fractions, ratios, percentages, algebra, area, speed, probability, sequences. Reading passage theme: determination and calm focus. Writing prompts like “The moment everything changed.” Reasoning includes patterns, analogies, logic, and sequences.

🧩 Reasoning Pattern Workbook

  • Number patterns: alternating, doubling/halving, prime jumps, square/cube sequences.
  • Verbal: synonyms, antonyms, analogies, categories, odd‑one‑out.
  • Non‑verbal: rotation, reflection, shading, shape addition/subtraction, progressive changes.
  • Logic: deduction, ordering, true/false tables, grid logic, conditional reasoning.
  • Mixed drills: exam‑style sets with step‑by‑step solutions.

✍️ Writing Templates & Rubric

Narrative Template (20 minutes)

  • Paragraph 1: Hook + setting + character
  • Paragraph 2: Problem / conflict
  • Paragraph 3: Turning point / decision
  • Paragraph 4: Resolution + reflection

Persuasive Template

  • Intro: clear position
  • Body 1: reason + example
  • Body 2: reason + example
  • Conclusion: restate + strong final line

Descriptive Template

  • Use 5 senses + emotion
  • End with a “zoom‑out” sentence

Marking Rubric (/10)

  • Content: /4 — strength of ideas and message
  • Structure: /2 — clear beginning, middle, end
  • Language: /2 — vocabulary + sentence variety
  • Mechanics: /2 — spelling, punctuation, grammar
